## Help! I'm not sure about things in ğŸ§¡ğŸ’»R for BABS1!

There will be people reading who missed workshops in semester 1 or who
want a refresher. If you mostly kept up but feel rusty now you can
probably just go though the materials as we did last semester trying out
things you have forgotten and skipping things where you feel more
confident.

If you missed most of the workshops or feel very behind this is the plan
I suggest. It focusses on the
[book](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/) and some of the
demonstrations I did at the start of workshops. I haven't included the
workshops so you won't get lots of practice but this list gives you good
bang-for-your-buck. You'll learn most of the ideas - without having to
solve workshop problems - in the shortest time.

1.  The most important thing to remember is that any progress, no matter
    how small, is better than none. It

    is always ok to be working on material from R for BABS1 in the R for
    BABS 2 workshops.

2.  Set up the [Virtual desktop
    service](https://3mmarand.github.io/R4BABS/r4babs1/week-6/workshop.html)
    so you can log into a university computer from any where. This means
    you won't have to install R and RStudio or any packages.
    Alternatively any time this week it should be easy to find a
    computer in the library, Biology R012 or anywhere else on campus

3.  You might want to look at the [questions and
    answers](menti%20questions%20and%20answers) from a menti in session
    introducing R for BABS1

4.  Read [What they forgot to teach you about
    computers](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/what-they-forgot.html) in [Computational
    Analysis for Bioscientists](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/)

5.  Read [What are R and
    Rstudio?](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/about-R-and-RStudio.html)

6.  Watch [Getting started with
    Rstudio](https://youtu.be/7oLLjkTNrjY?si=VQZ3LDnyR367qYDj) and [Data
    Structures and
    functions](youtube.com/watch?time_continue=2&v=2Ke1FGdaMSI&embeds_referring_euri=https%3A%2F%2F3mmarand.github.io%2F&source_ve_path=MzY4NDIsMjg2NjY).
    Or Read [Introduction to
    RStudio](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/about-R-and-RStudio.html#introduction-to-rstudio)
    and [First Steps in
    RStudio](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/first-steps-rstudio.html)
    if you prefer written material.

7.  Read [Workflow in
    RStudio](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/workflow-rstudio.html)

8.  Watch the demonstration at the start of the [Semester 1 week 7
    workshop](https://york.cloud.panopto.eu/Panopto/Pages/Viewer.aspx?id=fab41c28-1e28-4143-bfee-b38b00adc909)
    (this was the first workshop). It summarises the organisation of the
    materials, some of the ideas covered in the reading/watching above
    but also demonstrates changing the RStudio settings, and talks more
    about paths, shows project set up and so on. It might also help you
    decide if you would find it useful to do the workshop. Sorry -
    you'll have to log into the VLE to access that panopto link.

9.  Read [Understanding the
    pipe `|>`](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/workflow-rstudio.html#understanding-the-pipe)

10. Read [Ideas about
    data](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/ideas-about-data.html)

11. Read [From importing to
    reporting](https://3mmarand.github.io/comp4biosci/import-to-report.html).

12. Watch the demonstration at the start of the [Semester 1 week 8
    workshop](https://york.cloud.panopto.eu/Panopto/Pages/Viewer.aspx?id=9a511efe-b87b-45e1-9e7a-b39300ad6001)
    (this was the second workshop). It summarises some of the ideas
    covered in the reading/watching above but also demonstrates project
    set up and data import and discusses working directories and paths.
    Again, you'll have to log into the VLE to access that panopto link.
